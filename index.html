Carol is a writer and researcher interested in how technology and numerical languages generate sensations of and from near futures, and how these sensations, made objects, become political and legal cogs. 


She earned her MA from the Centre for Research Architecture at Goldsmiths, University of London. For her dissertation, she investigated Harvard University’s SCoPeX project, which is developing the first climate engineering outdoor experiment in the United States through sulphur injection in the stratosphere. This project interrogated the presuppositions that guide experimentation regulations on the basis of secular definitions of risk through a study of primary scientific materials, press, and international legislation. She holds a BA in Literature from Reed College.
